## Generative AI: principles 
(version 1.1)

1. Generative AI is a category of artificial intelligence technologies that are capable of generating text, images, video, audio, and other media. Generative AI technologies are increasingly capable of producing plausible outputs and have low barriers to access made possible by chatbot style interfaces where users can provide simple prompts as input.

2. Advocates of Generative AI technologies argue that they have the potential to radically transform all kinds of fields of human activity. Others have raised concerns around accuracy, bias, privacy, and ownership of IP.  It's important that the Foundation engages positively with the opportunities of Generative AI while also addressing the challenges and potential negative consequences. This relates to both how we use Generative AI to undertake our work and how we help young people understand these technologies and the role they can play in their lives. 

3. This document sets out some principles for how the Foundation will approach the use of Generative AI technologies and that provide the parameters for using Generative AI applications in our work. 

4. This is a fast-moving field and we will keep these principles under review. If you are thinking about utilising Generative AI applications, you should check the latest version of this document. 

### Principles 

5. **We will experiment with Generative AI technologies.** It is important that the Foundation engages proactively with Generative AI technologies and explores whether and how they could help us achieve our mission more effectively. While we need to have safeguards in place, these should be to enable and not to prevent experimentation and learning. You should consult the Chief Technology and Product Officer if you are considering using Generative AI technologies in our learning experiences or content. 

6. **We will be open and transparent.** We will be open and transparent about where we are using Generative AI technologies, both internally and externally. When we use Generative AI to substantively produce content or control the experience of our products, we will include a note that explains which tools we used and what contribution it made. We will capture and share learning about the use of Generative AI internally and externally. 

7. **We won't compromise on quality.** While we will use Generative AI technologies to support our work, a human must always take responsibility for ensuring that the finished product meets our high expectations for accuracy and quality. If you use Generative AI technologies to help you with your work, you are responsible for checking the accuracy and quality of its output. We do not use Generative AI images in place of real photographs or videos of humans. 

8. We will safeguard data and information. We will always exercise caution when inputting data and information of any kind into a Generative AI technology, particularly in relation to user data, colleagues' personal data, or commercially sensitive business information. We will always comply with our [Privacy Policy](https://www.raspberrypi.org/privacy/). Colleagues will be provided access to Generative AI tools where such information can be safely shared. See the approved services list below. Use of other tools for experimentation purposes is permitted but only with non-sensitive data or information.

### Approved services

9. GitHub Copilot is a tool to [improve programming productivity](https://github.com/features/copilot). Colleagues are permitted to use the service through our GitHub Business account. It’s important to ensure that code which constitutes the Foundation’s intellectual property is only used with this account for IP protection and copyright reasons.

10. Google Gemini is [an AI assistant](https://workspace.google.com/solutions/ai/) which is offered as part of Google Business Suite. The tool offers a chat-bot style interface integrated into products like Sheets, Docs and Slides to help with productivity such as analysing data or helping write or refine emails. There are also tools like [NotebookLM](https://notebooklm.google/) which are included as part of our Google Workspace subscription which are permitted.

11. ChatGPT Teams is a version of ChatGPT which [meets industry standard compliance requirements](https://openai.com/enterprise-privacy) and does not use input or output data to further train OpenAI’s models. We will conduct a small-scale trial to enable us to experiment with the ChatGPT and establish whether we should continue with the service in addition to Google Gemini. The IT team is in the process of procuring and rolling out the service to individuals put forward for the trial.

**Note ends.**
