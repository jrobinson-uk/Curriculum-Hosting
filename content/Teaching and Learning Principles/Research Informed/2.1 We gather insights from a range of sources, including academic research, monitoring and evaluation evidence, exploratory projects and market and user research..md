We use various sources to inform our work (fig. 1). This includes academic research, which provides a crucial evidence base. Recent years have seen a greater emphasis on research with school-aged learners, rather than undergraduates, providing more directly applicable findings.

We combine academic research with our own monitoring and evaluation data, using analytics and direct feedback to understand how our digital products are used and whether they are having the intended or desired impact.

User and market research helps us understand the specific needs and contexts of our audiences, enabling us to tailor our learning experiences to suit either a specific audience or be applicable to the widest audience possible.

Where evidence and empirical data are sparse, we will innovate and develop exploratory projects, piloting, testing, and learning from the outcome. Our skilled learning designers combine these insights alongside their experience and "craft knowledge"[^1]to design the very best learning experiences possible.
