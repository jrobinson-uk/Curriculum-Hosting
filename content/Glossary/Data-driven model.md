> [!secondary] Older Learners

> A representation of something in the real world which can be used to solve a problem. Data-driven models are created by identifying patterns in large amounts of training data. An AI chatbot uses a data-driven model, trained on many example conversations, to reply to users.

> [!educator] Educators
> 
> Data-driven is a way of designing systems using data instead of step-by-step instructions. For example, knowing what causes certain diseases is hard, but there is lots of example data. Therefore, designers use the medical data of people affected by the disease to diagnose it. Data-driven systems stand in contrast with rule-based systems. Data-driven systems are suitable for solving problems where rules that cover every situation are difficult to produce. Instead, enough examples can be collected to inform a solution.
